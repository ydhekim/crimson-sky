# Lore & Worldbuilding — Crimson Sky

Last updated: 2026-07-08

Source: translated, restructured, and renamed from the original Turkish draft ("Demir ve Avaz: Oyunun Kutsal Kitabı" / *Iron and Voice: The Lore Bible*, v1.0). That working title is retired — **Crimson Sky is the final global title**, and (happily) it already encodes the setting's core duality: the Crimson Accord vs. the Skyborn, per §4. All character, place, and faction names below are newly invented for this pass — the source draft drew directly on a real Turkish/Turkic epic (Köroğlu) and Tengrist cosmology (Gök/Ülgen vs. Yer/Erlik), which we're deliberately not citing verbatim so the setting stands on its own for a global audience. Structure and thematic beats are preserved; names, phrasing, and voice are new.

This is **moderate-depth worldbuilding**: enough to name things consistently (weapons, skills, pets, arenas, cosmetics) and give the two-faction structure a reason to exist — not a campaign or quest narrative. See §5 for how heavily this should surface in actual gameplay text.

---

## 1. Premise

Two First Powers wanted incompatible things from the same creation. Their compromise split every person into a body that must obey rules and a will that gets to gamble against them. The world's history is the argument over which half should win — and the Arena is where that argument now plays out forever, one duel at a time, because the alternative was letting it destroy everything else.

---

## 2. Cosmology: The Split (creation myth)

Before Time, Place, and Matter, there was only **the Essence** — at once a boundless **Depth** (potential, chaos, possibility) and the hard **Shore** that contained it (rule, order, form). When the Essence dreamed of a being that could exist inside the world *and* choose its own path, it hit a paradox:

- **The Depth's wish:** *"Let it be free as water."* Unlimited freedom has no shape — a being like that can't exist in the physical world at all. It becomes an absence: a **Ghost**.
- **The Shore's wish:** *"Let it be fixed as stone."* Unlimited rule has no will — a being like that isn't alive, just a **Statue**.

For a person to exist at all, the Essence tore itself in two, in pain:

1. **The Sky-Will** breathed the power of choice — chance, risk, the roll of the dice — into people.
2. **The Deep-Law** clothed people in death and limits — the fixed rules a body must obey.

> **What is a person?** A Ghost trapped inside a Statue, forever trying to slip its shape.

## 3. The Ages

### Age I — Steel and Oath

People forged iron but didn't worship it. A blade was an extension of the warrior's own body, a way of speaking to the First Powers.

- A formal duel between two champions was called **a Reckoning** — not hostility, but an offering.
- **The Verdict** (the root of what the codebase calls RNG): a Reckoning existed to reveal who the First Powers favored *that day*. A miss wasn't clumsiness — it was the Powers turning away. A critical, perfect strike was their blessing made visible.
- **The Open Guard:** true valor meant stepping into your opponent's range without knowing the outcome. A champion who never risked anything earned no favor at all — the Verdict doesn't answer a closed hand.

### Age II — Black Powder and Betrayal

The Deep-Law, sensing how much people feared *not knowing*, whispered the formula for black powder into the world.

- Smiths put down the blade and forged the **long-iron** (firearm).
- The break came the day a duelist fired from beyond range — no approach, no salute, no risk taken. No dice were rolled. No Verdict was asked for. The outcome was certain before the trigger was pulled.
- Chance was removed from the equation entirely. A Reckoning stopped being an offering and became an **execution**.

### Age III — Sight and Secret

Two opposite answers to the same fear (of an uncertain future) split the world's champions into two lines that still define the Arena today.

**The Warden and the Seer.** A warlord who wanted absolute, guaranteed order had his own farsighted stablehand — a man named **Yavor**, said to be able to see the outcome of any Reckoning before it happened — blinded, so that Yavor's stolen sight could be kept and used instead of trusted to Yavor himself.

- Yavor's eyes were sealed inside a shrine where the Warden's order is still ritually invoked.
- The Warden's soldiers wear **masks with no eye-holes**, consecrated with Yavor's own tears. Aiming through the mask means surrendering your own aim entirely to the Deep-Law's certainty — you will not miss, but you no longer choose where you strike. This submission is called **the Accord**.

**The Veiled Son.** Yavor's son fled to the wilderness — **the Hollow Pines** — to avenge his father. Drinking three draughts of foam from **the Silverfoam River**, he achieved a state called **the Secret**: a rejection of fixed matter entirely.

- His followers learned to unbind their own bodies into **mist and lightning** through will and risk alone.
- Because they are no longer fully material, the Warden's Sight cannot lock onto them — they can't be hit with certainty, only *maybe* hit, at the cost of never being fully solid or safe themselves. This defiance is called **the Wild Oath**.

### Age IV — The Age of the Scale (present day)

The Sky-Will and the Deep-Law, pushed to their absolute extremes by the two lines above, were about to collide hard enough to unmake the world. The First Powers intervened and froze that collision into a single place outside time: **the Arena** — which is, in-fiction, why the combat system is named the **Mizan** ("Scale"/"Balance") Combat Engine.

- **Embers** — sparks of the original Split, the same stuff people were made of in Age 0 — fall into the Arena from both the Sky and the Deep.
- **The Crimson Accord** (the Warden's line) must gather Embers to keep the masks' certainty burning. Let the fire die, and their soldiers go blind again.
- **The Skyborn** (the Veiled Son's line) must gather Embers to keep the Secret alive and, eventually, unmake the Deep-Law's grip on the world entirely.
- **The player** is a soul who has picked a side in this endless court: submit to the Accord and choose certainty, or take the Wild Oath and choose the miracle of chance.

## 4. The Two Factions — present-day identity

| | **The Crimson Accord** | **The Skyborn** |
|---|---|---|
| Founding figure | The Warden (unnamed lord, keeper of Yavor's Sight) | The Veiled Son (Yavor's avenging child) |
| Philosophy | Certainty through submission — surrender your will, never miss | Freedom through risk — keep your will, accept you might miss |
| Symbol | The eyeless mask | Mist and lightning |
| Feel | Disciplined, ritualistic, absolute | Wild, defiant, unstable |
| Color identity | Crimson / iron-dark | Sky-blue / storm-grey |

This is also the natural place to hang a faction-vs-faction mechanical identity — see §6.

## 5. Tone guidance (how this shows up in-game)

Per the "My Brute / Pockie Ninja" feel you described, this cosmology is **background canon**, not the voice players hear turn-to-turn:

- **Codex / lore entries** (something a curious player opts into reading): full mythic register, as written above.
- **In-match text** (action labels, victory/defeat screens, tooltips, loading tips): short, punchy, almost meme-able — closer to a stat-flex than scripture. Examples:
  - Instead of *"The masks have found their true path,"* → **"CRIT — dead on."**
  - Instead of *"Your will slipped from the Deep-Law's grasp,"* → **"DODGED."**
  - A loading tip can wink at the lore without performing it: *"Crimson never misses on purpose. Sky never stands still on purpose."*
- Faction names ("Crimson Accord," "Skyborn") are short enough to use directly in UI (faction badges, matchmaking labels) without translation or shortening.

## 6. Open hooks for other systems (not built yet — flagged, not designed here)

- **Faction skills (crit vs. dodge):** you proposed Crimson gets a skill that raises critical chance, Skyborn gets one that raises dodge chance, kept in balance. This maps cleanly onto the existing GDD stat table (STR/INT already governs "high-tier action weight," i.e. crit is a natural extension of that; SPD already governs dodge chance) — thematically it's a near-perfect fit (Crimson = certainty = "when you hit, you hit true"; Skyborn = evasion = "you're half mist, hard to pin down"). Recommend implementing this as **faction-gated `Skill` records** through the existing Loadout/Skill system rather than a new mechanic layer (reuses an existing pattern, per the codebase's own preference for extending rather than inventing). The actual numbers depend on the combat GDD detailing pass (there's currently no defined "critical hit" concept at all in the 3-page GDD) — logged as a forward pointer in `01-system-design-combat-engine.md` §14, not specced here.
- **Embers as an actual match resource:** the lore treats Embers as something both factions must "gather" — worth deciding later whether that's pure flavor for why the Arena exists, or an actual pickup/resource mechanic during a match. Not decided; flagged for whenever content/systems design revisits Epic J-style ideas.
- **Faction selection UX:** how/when a player picks Crimson vs. Skyborn (character creation? per-character? cosmetic-only vs. gameplay-binding?) isn't decided — depends on the answer to the skills question above. Worth knowing: `Character.faction` already exists in code today (a `Faction` enum, currently just placeholder `A`/`B`), so the scaffolding is further along than this doc alone would suggest — see `01-system-design-combat-engine.md` §14 for the rename flag.
